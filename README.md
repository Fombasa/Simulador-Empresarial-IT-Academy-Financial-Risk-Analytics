# Banking Risk & Data Analytics - IT Academy

## Project Context

Developed within a structured banking simulation, this project reflects the analytical workflow of a data team operating across risk, marketing, and customer strategy functions.


The project was structured in weekly business-driven sprints, where each analytical domain addressed specific strategic questions related to customer behavior, credit risk, and campaign effectiveness.

The objective was not only to analyze data, but to translate quantitative evidence into operational and strategic recommendations.

---

## Operational & Data Context

The simulation reproduces a real banking data lifecycle.

Customer interactions generate structured datasets including:

- Demographic and socio-economic variables  
- Financial balances and product holdings  
- Loan and mortgage exposure  
- Campaign contact history and conversion outcomes  

Additionally, external national datasets (INE projections and ECF survey data) were incorporated to align internal strategy with macro-level demographic and financial behavior trends.

---

## Analytical Domains & Business Focus

The project integrated three core analytical areas, each guided by concrete business questions.

### 1. Finance & Credit Risk

**Focus:**
- Assess the relationship between account balance levels and default probability  
- Analyze the interaction between product exposure (loans, mortgages) and risk  
- Identify operational balance thresholds indicating increased delinquency risk  

**Key work:**
- Balance segmentation and default rate concentration analysis
- Hypothesis testing to validate risk differentials across segments
- Interpretable logistic modeling for default probability assessment  
- Weighted regression analysis (WLS) for survey-based data  
- Translation of quantitative findings into risk policy recommendations

---

### 2️. Marketing & Communication

**Focus:**
- Optimize contact frequency to maximize campaign success  
- Evaluate channel impact (mobile vs telephone) on conversion rate  
- Assess weekday effects on campaign effectiveness  

**Key work:**
- Conversion rate monitoring and KPI governance  
- Contact-volume vs success rate analysis  
- Channel-based performance comparison  
- Business-aligned communication strategy proposals  

---

### 3️. Customer Profile & Behavioral Segmentation

**Focus:**
- Identify demographic combinations linked to multi-product usage  
- Detect structural differences in financial behavior across segments  
- Assess vulnerability patterns within population groups  

**Key work:**
- Socio-demographic segmentation  
- Multi-product behavioral profiling 
- Cross-variable interaction analysis 

### 4️. External Data Integration – INE & ECF Analysis

**Focus:**
- Align internal banking strategy with national demographic projections (INE)  
- Analyze financial competence and behavioral trends using ECF survey data  
- Identify structural population patterns relevant for long-term strategic planning  

**Key work:**
- Analysis of demographic projections from INE to evaluate future customer segments  
- Construction of composite indicators based on ECF (financial competence, financial stress, vulnerability)  
- Survey-weighted econometric modeling (Logit & WLS)  
- Population-level inference to support strategic product and risk adaptation  

---

## Methodological Approach

The project emphasizes statistical rigor and interpretability.

Methods applied include:

- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)  
- Data cleaning and structured dataset preparation  
- Non-parametric statistical testing (Chi-square, Kruskal–Wallis)  
- Logistic regression (Logit models)  
- Weighted Least Squares (WLS)  
- Threshold identification for operational decision-making  
- Clear distinction between association and causality  

The analytical focus prioritizes robust, interpretable methods suitable for governance and risk environments.
